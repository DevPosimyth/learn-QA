# How to Create Templates for Private Use?

## Overview

WDesignKit enables users to establish templates exclusively for personal use or restricted group access. Templates remain in private mode by default when exported from Elementor or Gutenberg.

## Default Privacy Status

When exporting templates through WDesignKit, templates remain in **private mode by default**. No additional action is required to maintain privacy initially.

---

## Converting a Public Template Back to Private

If a template was previously made public, you can revert it to private status:

1. Navigate to the WDesignKit plugin page
2. Go to **Templates > My Templates**
3. Locate the desired template
4. Click the **three dots menu**
5. Select **Edit**
6. On the WDesignKit app page, access the **Visibility** dropdown
7. Choose **Private**
8. Click the **Save** button

The same process is available from the WDesignKit app under **Templates > Uploaded** page.

---

## Benefits of Private Templates

- **Personal use across websites** — Utilize templates on multiple sites you manage
- **Restricted sharing** — Share privately with specific team members
- **Workspace collaboration** — Create a workspace to share with selected individuals

---

## Requirements

This feature requires the WDesignKit plugin to be installed and activated. Users must also be logged in to both the WDesignKit website and plugin page.
